Welcome to the Cat Photography Tips Website

On this site you will find some useful tips on how to take beautiful photos with your cellphone camera of your cat or other pets! As the occurence of social media has exploded during the resent decades, and as our cellphones has been equipped with more and more improved cameras, this is a time where we all want to take beautiful pictures to share with our friends and family.

Tip #1 Focus point

The first tip is to get the focus right, where in the photo the focus point is set, it will be sharp. Have you ever tried to take a close-up photo of your animal, ending up getting a very blurry furball somewhere and a very sharp background in the photo? This is because the camera has set it's focus in the background. On most modern day cellphones, as you start your camera, you can touch the screen and the auto-focus will shift to the spot where you pointed. Have a go and see if your focus point shifts! PS: It can sometimes be better not to be too close to the object you focus on, start with 50cm (20") and see how close you can come with your camera.DS.

Tip #2 Wright with light

Photo stands for Light and Graphy stands for Write. In other words photography menas "to wright with light". By default your camera tries to find the "middle" of the light resources avaliable, this means for insance, if your cat is sitting in the window on a bright and sunny day, and you are behind your cat, trying to take a photo. The camera "wants" both the sunlit outdoor fields and sky as well as the inside of your window and your perhaps dark cat to have the same "exposure value". Therefore you end up with a too dark image where you see the outside clouds, but your cat is a bit too dark. On most modern cellphone-camera-applications you can set the auto exposure point just as easily as you set your focus. Did you notice in the previous tip, as you set your focus point, it appearing a line with a dot in the middle and a sun-symbol? If you press on the dot and pull it up or down or side to side, depending on your camera application, this will change the amount of light your camera letts in. In other words, your black cat in the window might be 'purrfectly' lit, but your outside view might be as one say "burned out". Now try to adjust the light setting as well as the focus point. You can also experiment with the flash, if you want to keep the background AND the motive well lit. But my advice to you is not to use the flash close to your animal! but instead let the natural light illuminate your photos. PS: Toggle off the automatic flash by pressing the flash icon that is crossed over. DS.

Tip #3 Rule of thirds and rulers to follow

Ever heard of the rule of thirds? that states that an image should be "divided" into nine equally big squares? For instance the horizon could be aligned along the "dividing line" between the two upper lines of squares and the line of squares on the bottom, thus creating a more harmonius image rather than had it been in the exact middle of the photo. In your phone's camera application, you will hopefully find the setting where you can see lines on the screen as you line up your camera to take the photo. This could help you line up your photo. But then again, maby some rules are made to be broken? See if you find the setting where you can see the lines / grid as you take your photo.

Tip #4 Harvest the Crop

Now that you have taken your photography to a new level, do not be afraid to experiment. My advice to you is to take lots and lots of photos and then, (when the cat gets tired of you and leave the well lit window) you can look at the photos in your gallery and choose which to save and which to delete. Now is also the time to look at the functions in your gallery-application. Here, often the function-icon, has the form of a pencil, you will find that you can edit your photos once more with diffrent filters and light settings. Play around with these but try not to add too much sharpening, it is better to have set the focus as described in the first tip. Here however, you will also find the crop-function. the function icon often depicted as two angles lined up as a square with dotted lines fron where the lines meet. This is very useful so that you can straighten up your photos. and also flip them 90° at a time if you see needed. Go on and test out the functions of your gallery application.
